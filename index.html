Come on, we know about Bandarban

Bandarban is a district in southeastern Bangladesh, located in the Chittagong Hill Tracts. Known for its breathtaking natural beauty, diverse tribal cultures, and tranquil mountain landscapes, Bandarban is a popular destination for nature lovers, hikers, and cultural explorers.

  • Nilgiri Hills A popular tourist spot with panoramic views.
  • Nilachal Offers a birds-eye view of the Bandarban town.
  • Chimbuk Hill Third-highest mountain in Bangladesh, accessible by jeep

Going to Sylhet

Sylhet is a major metropolitan city serving as the administrative center of Sylhet District and Sylhet Division. It sits on the banks of the Surma River and had an estimated metro population of 1,033,000 in 2025—making it the countrys third-largest urban area after Dhaka and Chittagong Historically occupied by various dynasties including the Mughals, the British colonials, and the Nawabs of Bengal, Sylhet also acts as a pilgrimage center due to Sufi shrines such as Shah Jalal Dargah and Shah Paran Dargah, which draw thousands throughout the year

  • Malnicherra Tea Estate: Established in 1849, its the oldest tea garden in the Indian subcontinent, spanning roughly 1,500 acres and attracting scores of visitors daily
  • Ratargul Swamp Forest: Bangladesh’s only freshwater swamp forest, offering a rare ecosystem flooded during monsoon and home to diverse wildlife
  • Surrounding highlights: Locations such as Sreemangal, Jaflong, Lalakhal, Lawachara, Baikka Beel, Hum Hum Waterfall, and Shunamganj haors are frequently mentioned by travelers for their natural charm
